Risk Disclosure & Legal Warning

Last updated: May 31, 2026

Trading financial assets carries a high level of risk and may not be suitable for all investors. Before deciding to trade foreign exchange (Forex), contract for differences (CFDs), or virtual digital assets (Cryptocurrency), you should carefully consider your investment objectives, level of experience, and risk appetite.

1. General High-Risk Warning & Leverage

Leveraged trading allows you to control larger positions with a relatively small deposit (margin). While leverage can amplify profits, it can equally amplify losses. It is possible to lose all of your initial capital. You should never trade with funds that you cannot afford to lose, such as household savings, emergency reserves, or borrowed funds.

2. Specific Forex Regulations in India (FEMA Guidelines)

Important Notice for Indian Residents

Under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) and directives issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), resident Indians are strictly prohibited from trading currency pairs that do not involve the Indian Rupee (INR) on unauthorized electronic trading portals (such as MT4/MT5 platforms through international retail brokers). Resident Indians are only permitted to trade currency derivatives on SEBI-authorized domestic exchanges (NSE, BSE, MSE) through SEBI-registered brokers, and all currency pairs must involve the INR.

3. Specific Cryptocurrency Volatility & Taxation in India

Virtual Digital Assets (VDAs) or Cryptocurrencies are highly volatile and operate on decentralized networks. Cryptocurrency trading is subject to significant market fluctuations, liquidity events, and security risks. You must be aware of the following legal framework inside India:

  • Taxation (Section 115BBH): As of the Income Tax Act guidelines, income from the transfer of VDAs is taxed at a flat rate of 30%. No deductions for expenses (other than cost of acquisition) or offsetting of losses between different assets are permitted.
  • TDS (Section 194S): A 1% TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is applicable on the transfer of Virtual Digital Assets on exchanges.
  • Unregulated Status: VDAs are currently unregulated in India. Regulatory policies are subject to change at any moment by government bodies, which may severely restrict trading liquidity or access.
